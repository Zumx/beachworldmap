The United States has tropical, temperate, and wild coastlines across two
oceans, a gulf, and a chain of islands. These ten beaches are the standouts.

## 1. Lanikai Beach, Oʻahu, Hawaii

Powder sand and impossibly turquoise water facing offshore islets — a Pacific
benchmark.

## 2. Siesta Key, Florida

Quartz sand so fine and white it stays cool underfoot — a Gulf Coast classic.

## 3. Cannon Beach, Oregon

Dramatic sea stacks and moody Pacific surf — wild, photogenic, and cool.

## 4. Coronado Beach, San Diego

Wide golden sand with mineral sparkle and a landmark hotel backdrop.

## 5. Cape Cod National Seashore, Massachusetts

Miles of protected dune-backed Atlantic beach with classic New England light.

## 6. Hāpuna Beach, Big Island, Hawaii

A broad white-sand crescent on a mostly volcanic coast.

## 7. Driftwood Beach, Jekyll Island, Georgia

A surreal shore of weathered, bleached trees — a photographer's beach.

## 8. Pfeiffer Beach, Big Sur, California

Purple-tinged sand and a sea arch on the most dramatic coast in the lower 48.

## 9. South Beach, Miami, Florida

Art Deco backdrop, warm Atlantic water — beach culture as spectacle.

## 10. Ruby Beach, Washington

Sea stacks, driftwood, and tide pools on the wild Olympic Peninsula.

## How US beaches work

Many of the best sit within national or state seashores and parks with entry
fees, parking limits, and protected dunes — arrive early in summer. Lifeguard
coverage is seasonal and patchy outside major resort beaches; rip currents are
the leading hazard nationwide. Hawaii's shore breaks and Pacific Northwest
sneaker waves deserve specific respect.

## When to go

It spans climates: Hawaii and Florida are year-round (watch hurricane season,
roughly June–November, in the Atlantic and Gulf); California and the Northeast
peak in summer; the Pacific Northwest is about drama, not swimming, and is best
in late summer.

## Stay safe and leave no trace

Learn to spot and escape rip currents (swim parallel to shore), heed posted
flags, protect yourself from strong UV, stay off and behind protected dunes,
and pack out all litter. Tide-pool and wildlife areas are fragile — look, do
not take.
