Some beaches are so improbable they barely look real — sand the wrong colour,
hidden inside rock, or scattered with diamonds of glacier ice. These are the
world's beach icons.

## Whitehaven Beach, Australia

Near-pure silica sand swirling into turquoise at Hill Inlet — for many the most
beautiful beach on Earth.

## Hidden Beach (Playa del Amor), Mexico

A circular beach inside a collapsed crater on the Marietas Islands, reached by
swimming through a tunnel.

## Anse Source d'Argent, Seychelles

Sculpted granite boulders, shallow turquoise water, and palm shade — the most
photographed tropical beach in the world.

## Reynisfjara, Iceland

Black volcanic sand, basalt columns, and Atlantic fury — beauty that is
dramatic rather than gentle.

## Diamond Beach, Iceland

Glacier ice washed onto black sand, glittering like jewels at the foot of a
lagoon.

## Navagio (Shipwreck) Beach, Greece

A white cove with a rusted wreck, walled by sheer cliffs, reached only by boat.

## Pink Beach, Komodo, Indonesia

Coral fragments tint the sand rose against vivid blue water.

## Grace Bay, Turks and Caicos

A long, flawless reef-protected strip routinely topping global beach rankings.

## Hyams Beach, Australia

Famous for some of the whitest sand recorded, on Jervis Bay.

## Cala Macarella, Menorca, Spain

A perfect Mediterranean cove of pale sand and impossibly clear water.

## What makes a beach beautiful

Sometimes it is colour that should not exist (pink, black, glittering white),
sometimes geology that frames it (granite boulders, cliff walls, ice), and
sometimes water so clear it removes the horizon. The greatest beaches are
landscapes, not just swimming spots.

## Visiting the icons responsibly

Fame is pressure. Many of these limit numbers, require boats or permits, or
have closed periodically to recover from over-tourism (Maya Bay's lesson hangs
over all of them). Go in shoulder season, arrive early, take nothing — no
sand, shells, or "just one" ice diamond — stay off dunes and reef, and use
operators who respect the caps. These places stay beautiful only if visitors
let them.
