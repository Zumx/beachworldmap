The gap between the beach in the brochure and the beach you arrive at is
usually a planning failure — wrong season, wrong tide, wrong wind. A little
research turns a gamble into the trip you pictured.

## Step 1: Match the destination to the season

Beach destinations have sharp seasons: tropical coasts have wet and dry
seasons and storm/hurricane windows; temperate beaches peak in summer; some of
the best are off-season secrets. Research the specific coast's calm-and-dry
window — and avoid its storm season — before booking anything.

## Step 2: Map the coast

Open the [map](/), filter to your region, and look at how beaches cluster. A
base within reach of several beaches lets you pick the sheltered side
whatever the wind does that day — far better than committing to one exposed
beach.

## Step 3: Plan around wind and tide

Two overlooked variables: wind direction decides which beaches are calm or
choppy on a given day, and tide can turn a vast beach into a sliver (or cut
off a cove entirely). Check a tide table and the wind forecast and plan which
beach for which day accordingly.

## Step 4: Build a safety plan

Identify which beaches are patrolled and when. Learn rip-current spotting and
escape before you travel, note the local emergency number, and check for
region-specific hazards — marine stingers, strong shore breaks, sneaker waves,
crocodiles in some tropics. Safety is a plan, not a hope.

## Step 5: Sort logistics early

Popular beaches have limited parking that fills by mid-morning and may charge
or require booking; some protected seashores cap daily visitors. Plan early
arrivals, know the facilities (toilets, shade, water — often none), and pack to
be self-sufficient.

## Step 6: Pack for sun and self-sufficiency

Shade shelter, high-SPF sunscreen plus cover-up clothing, far more water than
feels necessary, a first-aid kit, and a dry bag. Sun and dehydration, not the
sea, end most beach days early.

## Step 7: Have a weather backup

A single rigid beach day is hostage to the forecast. Keep an alternative
sheltered beach and a non-beach wet-weather option so a bad day is a swap, not
a write-off.

## Step 8: Leave it as you found it

Pack out everything, stay off dunes, avoid reef and wildlife contact, and use
reef-safe sunscreen near coral. The beach economy and ecology both depend on
visitors treading lightly.

## Put it together

Season, then map, then wind and tide, then safety, then logistics. Open the
[map](/), cluster the coast, and a beach trip becomes the postcard rather than
a gamble.
