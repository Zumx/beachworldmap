Hainan Island lies at the southern tip of mainland China, separated from Guangdong Province by the narrow Qiongzhou Strait. At roughly 33,900 square kilometres it is China's second-largest island after Taiwan, and it became a Special Economic Zone in 1988, a status that accelerated the construction of resort infrastructure along its southern coast. The provincial capital Haikou sits at the northern tip; the resort city of Sanya anchors the south and is the hub of Hainan's beach tourism. The island sits between the 18th and 20th parallels north and has a genuinely tropical climate — summer temperatures exceed 30 degrees Celsius from May through October, and even January rarely falls below 20 degrees. The Chinese government has been positioning Hainan as a free-trade port, which has further accelerated development and investment since 2020. ## 1. Yalong Bay, Sanya

Yalong Bay is the most developed and internationally marketed beach in Sanya, a 7.5-kilometre crescent that has been the site of sustained large-scale hotel development since the early 1990s. The beach strip now hosts numerous international hotel brands, and the bay is bookended by a national holiday resort park. The water is clear and the sand is fine and pale. Visibility for snorkelling and diving is good in the mornings before boat traffic increases. Coral at the bay's southern headland has suffered from development pressure but isolated patches remain.

## 2. Dadonghai Beach, Sanya

Closer to Sanya city centre than Yalong Bay, Dadonghai is a shorter beach — roughly 1.5 kilometres — with a more local and mid-market character. It has been a beach destination since before the major resort build-out and retains a mix of domestic Chinese holiday-makers, Russian tourists who have been visiting since the 1990s, and younger visitors attracted by its proximity to Sanya's restaurants and nightlife. The beach is well maintained, with regular cleaning and a beach volleyball court.

## 3. Sanya Bay Beach (Sanya Wan)

Sanya Bay stretches for about 22 kilometres along the western edge of the Sanya peninsula and faces southwest. It is the longest beach in the Sanya area and the most convenient to the city's train station. The beach lacks the concentrated resort development of Yalong Bay and has more of an urban beach character, with the Luhuitou peninsula closing the southern end of the bay. Coconut palms have been planted along the promenade in large numbers and the seafront road is a popular cycling and walking route.

## 4. 爱情沙滩 (Love Beach), Sanya Area

Love Beach, whose name translates literally as Romance Beach or Love Beach, is a smaller cove-style beach southeast of Sanya marketed primarily to Chinese couples and honeymooners. The beach is compact and photogenic, with clear water in calm weather, and it has been the setting for numerous Chinese film and television productions. The sheltered aspect means the water is typically calm even when swells affect the more exposed beaches. Infrastructure is modest — a few food stalls and sunbed hire.

## 5. 阳光沙滩 (Sunshine Beach), Sanya Region

Sunshine Beach occupies a stretch of the southeast Hainan coast and is one of several beaches in the Sanya municipality developed as day-trip and weekend destinations for Sanya city residents rather than international resort visitors. The beach is wide, the sand is clean, and the facilities are basic by resort standards. It is a good option for visitors wanting to escape the concentrated hotel zones of Yalong Bay and Dadonghai and find a more relaxed atmosphere.

## 6. 大灣 Tai Wan (Great Bay), East Hainan

On the less-developed eastern coast, Great Bay (Tai Wan) sits in a region of Hainan that sees significantly fewer visitors than the southern resort zone. The eastern coast faces the South China Sea directly and receives stronger wind and surf than the sheltered southern bays, making it more interesting for surfing but requiring more caution for casual swimming. The surrounding landscape retains more of the traditional Hainan village character than the resort-heavy south, with small fishing communities working from the beach.

## 7. 東灣 Tung Wan (East Bay), East Hainan

East Bay is another of the quieter east-coast beaches, backed by coconut plantation and low scrub. The driving distance from Sanya means it falls well outside the main tourist circuit and is primarily used by local residents. The beach is long and relatively undisturbed, and the water is cleaner than many beaches in the developed south because it is not surrounded by hotels and construction. Visiting requires a hired car or motorcycle, as public transport connections are limited.

## 8. 成功海灘 (Chenggong Beach), Northeast Hainan

Chenggong Beach lies in the northeast of the island and is associated with the small port town of the same name. Like the east-coast beaches, it benefits from distance from the Sanya resort zone and the consequent lower visitor numbers. The beach has a working harbour adjacent, which gives it an authentic character absent from the pure resort stretches. Fresh fish and seafood are available at the dock-side restaurants. The beach itself is wide at low tide.

## 9. 浪芒灣 Long Mong Wan (Rough Mane Bay), West Hainan

The western coast of Hainan faces into the Gulf of Tonkin, a sea area bordered by Vietnam to the west and separated from the South China Sea proper. Long Mong Wan is one of the bays on this coast, where the combination of calmer waters, mangrove stretches, and very limited tourism infrastructure creates a distinctive coastal character quite different from the Sanya resort strip. The west coast is the least developed part of Hainan for tourism but is significant economically for its petroleum industry.

## 10. 庐山沙海 (Lushan Sand Sea), Central Hainan

The Lushan Sand Sea is an anomaly in a tropical island context — a landscape of inland coastal dunes that developed through a combination of coastal sand supply and prevailing wind patterns. It is not a traditional beach destination but functions as a sand-dune adventure recreation area, with sandboarding and 4WD vehicles operating on the dunes. The nearest actual beach is several kilometres away, but the sand sea is a genuine geological curiosity and a popular day trip from Haikou in the north.

## Explore Hainan by Map

Hainan's beaches spread across a diverse island, from the hyper-developed south around Sanya to the almost entirely undeveloped east and west coasts. The distances are manageable — the island's expressway ring makes it possible to drive from Haikou to Sanya in roughly three hours — and an island circuit reveals coastal landscapes that the resort marketing rarely depicts.
