Central Vietnam's coastline runs for several hundred kilometres between the mountain spine of the Truong Son range and the South China Sea. Unlike the heavily promoted resorts of Phu Quoc or Nha Trang, this stretch belongs to a different register — one shaped by ancient trading ports, imperial citadels, and fishing communities that have worked these waters for centuries. The beaches here tend toward wide, pale-sand stretches separated by rocky headlands, and they reward travellers who take the time to move beyond the obvious. The best season runs from February through August; the coast faces northeast monsoon rains and occasional typhoons from September through January, so timing matters. ## 1. My Khe Beach, Da Nang

My Khe is the closest major beach to Da Nang city, stretching roughly 30 kilometres along the coast south of the Han River mouth. American soldiers called it China Beach during the war, and its wide, gently shelving sand and consistent surf made it a popular rest destination. Today it is lined with hotels and seafood restaurants, and it hosts a strong surfing community from September through December when swells arrive from the northeast. The water is warm for swimming from April to August.

## 2. Non Nuoc Beach, Da Nang

Immediately south of My Khe and separated from it by the Marble Mountains — a cluster of five limestone and marble hills honeycombed with pagodas and caves — Non Nuoc Beach is calmer and slightly less developed. The Marble Mountains rise directly behind the beach and are worth a morning visit before the tour buses arrive. Non Nuoc runs for about 5 kilometres and has some of Da Nang's better mid-range beach hotels. Stone-carving workshops at the base of the mountains have operated here for generations.

## 3. My An Beach, Da Nang

Between Non Nuoc and the Da Nang resort strip, My An is a neighbourhood beach with a quieter character than the main tourist areas. It is popular with local families in the early morning and late afternoon, and several Vietnamese seafood restaurants face the sea directly here. The beach is clean and lifeguarded during peak season. It represents a more everyday slice of Da Nang beach life than the international resort stretch further north.

## 4. Cua Dai Beach, Hoi An

Cua Dai sits about 4 kilometres east of Hoi An's ancient town and for many years served as the main beach for visitors to the UNESCO-listed trading port. The beach suffered significant erosion from the mid-2010s onward — a combination of upstream dam construction on the Thu Bon River and hotel development — and parts of it have narrowed dramatically. However, the offshore islands of the Cham archipelago (accessible by boat from Cua Dai pier) offer excellent snorkelling, and the beach still functions as a pleasant end point for a cycling trip from Hoi An's old town through rice paddies and river villages.

## 5. Gio Hai Beach, Quang Tri Province

North of Hue, Gio Hai is the kind of beach that rewards the effort to reach it. The fishing village at Gio Linh district is not heavily touristed, and the beach itself is wide and clean, backed by casuarina pines. The dunes here form part of a landscape shaped by the fierce coastal winds that characterise this stretch of Vietnamese coast. It is a working fishing community, and mornings bring the spectacle of round coracle basket boats returning through the surf with their catch.

## 6. Thuan An Beach, Thua Thien Hue Province

Thuan An is the closest beach to Hue city, about 13 kilometres northeast of the imperial capital. It sits on a narrow barrier spit between Tam Giang Lagoon — one of the largest lagoons in Southeast Asia at over 200 square kilometres — and the sea. The combination of lagoon kayaking and beach swimming makes it a full day trip, and the seafood restaurants along the beach road serve exceptionally fresh river prawn and mud crab sourced directly from the lagoon's fishing families.

## 7. Lang Co Beach, Thua Thien Hue Province

Lang Co is a peninsula of sand separating Lap An Lagoon from the sea, lying at the base of the Hai Van Pass. The pass itself was a strategic military and cultural boundary for centuries — the northern limit of historic Champa territory and later the boundary between Hue and Da Nang provinces. The beach stretches for about 10 kilometres, the water is shallow and warm, and the lagoon behind offers flat-water swimming and boat hire. The fishing village of Lang Co has a modest selection of guesthouses and some excellent local restaurants.

## 8. Sam Son Beach, Thanh Hoa Province

Sam Son is one of Vietnam's oldest beach resorts, developed during the French colonial period in the early 20th century when it served as a seaside retreat for Hanoi's administrative class. It lies about 16 kilometres east of Thanh Hoa city and has the somewhat faded grandeur of a resort that was once grander than its current infrastructure suggests. The beach is long and wide, the surf is moderate, and the area draws large numbers of domestic tourists from Hanoi during summer weekends. An offshore rock formation called Hon Trong Mai (Rooster and Hen Rocks) is a local landmark.

## 9. Bai Dai Beach, Phu Yen Province

South of the traditional central Vietnam region but part of the same coastal character, Bai Dai (Long Beach) in Phu Yen Province is one of the most visually striking stretches on the entire Vietnamese coast. The beach runs for over 20 kilometres between the Vung Ro headland and the Tuy Hoa River mouth, and its combination of consistent surf, pine forest backing, and clear water has made it a magnet for Vietnamese film productions. International tourism infrastructure is still minimal compared to Da Nang, which means the beach remains uncrowded on most days.

## 10. Doc Let Beach, Khanh Hoa Province

Doc Let is a 15-kilometre crescent of very fine white sand about 50 kilometres north of Nha Trang, separated from the main highway by a peninsula that keeps it sheltered and relatively private. The water is exceptionally clear and calm — suitable for families with young children — and the lack of large-scale hotel development means it retains a quieter atmosphere than beaches further south. Mud baths and salt flats in the surrounding area are part of the local economy.
