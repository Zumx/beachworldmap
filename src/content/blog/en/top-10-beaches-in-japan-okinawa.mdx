Okinawa Prefecture encompasses the Ryukyu Islands, a chain of more than 160 islands stretching roughly 1,000 kilometres southwest from Kyushu to Taiwan. The main island, Okinawa Honto, is home to the prefectural capital Naha and the majority of the population; the outer islands — the Kerama group, the Miyako Islands, and the Yaeyama Islands — are remote enough to require additional flights or long ferry journeys but reward the effort with some of the finest beaches and coral reefs in Japan. The sub-tropical climate keeps temperatures above 20 degrees Celsius even in January, and the official beach season runs from April through October. Typhoons can affect the islands from June through October. [Open the map](/map) for a geographic overview of the entire Ryukyu chain before planning which islands to visit.

## 1. 竜宮浜海水浴場 (Ryuguhama Beach), Itoman City

Ryuguhama Beach lies on the southern coast of Okinawa's main island near Itoman City, a fishing community at the southern tip of the island. The name refers to the underwater dragon palace of Ryugu-jo in Japanese folklore, reflecting the beach's exceptionally clear water. The southern main island coastline is generally less developed than the central resort zone around Naha and the American base communities, and Ryuguhama has a local rather than resort character. The beach is sheltered behind a fringing reef and the snorkelling on the reef edge is accessible by a short swim.

## 2. 野原海水浴場 (Nohara Beach), Naha Area

Nohara is one of the more accessible beaches from Naha's city centre and functions as an urban beach serving the capital. It has the infrastructure and maintained character of a Japanese municipal beach — regular cleaning schedules, flagged swimming zones, lifeguards during the official season, and adjacent parking and changing facilities. The water quality is maintained and the beach provides a quick retreat from Naha's dense urban fabric. It is significantly more crowded on weekends from June through August.

## 3. 岩瀬浜海水浴場 (Iwasehama Beach), Central Okinawa

Iwasehama Beach sits in the central zone of Okinawa's main island and provides a counterpoint to the international resort beaches of the Chatan and Onna coastline. The beach has a functional, community-oriented character and is popular with local families. The waters behind the fringing reef here are calm enough for children, and the reef itself is accessible for snorkelling. The beach road has several casual Okinawan restaurants serving the local staples of chanpuru stir-fries and Orion beer.

## 4. 阿字ヶ浦海水浴場 (Ajigaura Beach), Northern Okinawa Area

Ajigaura is a beach associated with the quieter northern reaches of Okinawa's main island, where the Yambaru forest comes close to the coast and the development pressure of the central resort zone gives way to a more traditional landscape. The beach is used by local residents and the smaller number of tourists who venture north of the resort strip. The Yambaru region of northern Okinawa was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2021 for its endemic species, and the coastal character here is part of a broader protected natural zone.

## 5. 出戸浜海水浴場 (Dedehama Beach), East Okinawa

The east coast of Okinawa's main island faces the Pacific and is significantly less developed for tourism than the west coast, which faces the East China Sea. Dedehama Beach is on this less-visited eastern shore. East coast beaches often have stronger wave action than the sheltered west coast and are more popular with surfing communities. The scenery is wilder, with headlands and small offshore islands breaking up the coastline.

## 6. 釜谷浜海水浴場 (Kamatanihama Beach), Remote Island

Kamatanihama Beach represents the style of coastal environment found on Okinawa's smaller inhabited outer islands — stripped of resort infrastructure, reached by ferry or small plane, with a beach quality determined entirely by natural conditions rather than development investment. The beach is known locally for its clear water and the fringing coral that provides snorkelling directly from the shore without the need for boat excursions.

## 7. 浜当目海水浴場 (Hamaatome Beach), Outer Island

Hamaatome Beach is another outer island beach in the Ryukyu chain offering the quiet and natural quality that the main island's resort coastline has largely traded away. The beach setting combines sand, forest backing, and accessible reef in the typical Okinawan configuration that makes the prefecture's beaches attractive. Facilities are minimal and this is the point — visitors arrive looking for peace, good water, and intact coral rather than hotel amenities.

## 8. 有珠海水浴場 (Usuumi Beach), Kerama Islands

The Kerama Islands, a group lying approximately 30 kilometres west of Naha and accessible by high-speed ferry in about 50 minutes, are considered the premier snorkelling and diving destination in the Ryukyu chain. The term Kerama Blue — keramanchi in the local dialect — refers to the extraordinary turquoise-blue clarity of the water around the islands, a product of their relatively undisturbed reef systems and position in strong current-bearing channels. Usuumi Beach on one of the Kerama islands is one of the accessible snorkelling beaches in this protected marine area.

## 9. 刈尾海水浴場 (Kario Beach), Miyako Islands

The Miyako Islands, about 290 kilometres southwest of the main island, are famous among Japanese beach travellers for some of the clearest water in the country. Yonaha Maehama Beach on Miyako-jima is consistently voted the best beach in Japan in domestic surveys, and Kario Beach represents the same extraordinary water clarity found throughout the Miyako group. The coral reef systems here survived the bleaching episodes of the late 1990s and early 2000s better than many other parts of Japan.

## 10. 検見川の浜 (Kemigawa Beach), Yaeyama Islands

The Yaeyama Islands form the southwestern extremity of the Ryukyu chain, with Iriomote-jima — a UNESCO World Heritage island largely covered in sub-tropical jungle and mangrove — and Taketomi-jima, a tiny coral island famous for its preserved Ryukyuan village architecture and star-sand beaches. Kemigawa Beach in the Yaeyama area represents the outer edge of the Okinawan beach landscape, where travel from Tokyo requires two flights and beaches are likely to be shared with a handful of other visitors at most.

## Navigate the Ryukyu Chain

The geography of Okinawa's beaches is not easily grasped from a list — the difference between a beach on the main island and one on the Kerama Islands or the Yaeyama group is the difference between a beach resort and a genuine wilderness experience.
