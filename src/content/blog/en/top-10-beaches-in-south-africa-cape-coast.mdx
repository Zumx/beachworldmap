The Cape Peninsula and its surrounding coastline presents one of the more unusual beach geographies in the world — a narrow sliver of land where two ocean systems meet, producing dramatically different beach environments within a few kilometres of each other. The Atlantic seaboard, on the western side of the Peninsula, faces into the cold Benguela Current, which upwells cold water from the deep ocean floor along the South African west coast. Sea temperatures here rarely exceed 16 degrees Celsius even in midsummer. The False Bay coast on the eastern side of the Peninsula sees water significantly warmer, typically 20 to 22 degrees Celsius in summer, as the Agulhas Current brings Indian Ocean water around Cape Point. The result is a coastline where wind, surf, water temperature, and scenery vary dramatically from beach to beach. The best swimming weather runs from November through March, though the Cape Doctor — the southeast wind that scours the city — can make conditions unpredictable on any summer afternoon. ## 1. Camps Bay Beach, Atlantic Seaboard

Camps Bay is the most photographed beach in Cape Town — a wide strip of white sand backed by the curved cliffs of the Twelve Apostles mountain range, overlooked by a promenade of restaurants and bars, and facing west so that sunsets are a daily spectacle. The water is cold enough to take the breath away, typically around 14 degrees Celsius in summer. Most visitors wade rather than swim seriously, but surfing and bodyboarding are possible. The beach is busy from October through March and the promenade restaurants are among the best in the city.

## 2. Glen Beach, Atlantic Seaboard

Glen Beach is a small pocket beach at the northern end of Camps Bay, tucked between the main Camps Bay strip and the Maiden's Cove tidal pools. It is popular with surfers when a northwest swell is running and is consistently considered one of the better surfing beaches on the Peninsula's Atlantic side. The beach is small enough that it becomes crowded on summer weekends, but it has a distinct community character compared to the tourist-heavy main Camps Bay beach.

## 3. Grotto Beach, Hermanus

Hermanus is a coastal town about 120 kilometres east of Cape Town on the southern shore of Walker Bay, and Grotto Beach is the main beach — a 12-kilometre stretch of Blue Flag certified sand backed by the De Mond coastal reserve. Hermanus is internationally famous as a land-based whale watching destination: Southern right whales enter Walker Bay from July through November to calve, and they can be watched from the cliff paths above the beach or from the beach itself. The sea at Grotto Beach is cleaner and warmer than the Atlantic seaboard beaches of Cape Town.

## 4. Voëlklip Beach, Hermanus

Voëlklip sits on the rocky eastern edge of Hermanus, at the foot of the famous cliff path that runs for 12 kilometres along the Walker Bay shoreline. The name means Bird's Cliff in Afrikaans. The beach is smaller than Grotto and slightly more exposed, but it is backed by the old fishing harbour and has an informal character that some visitors prefer to the more developed main beach. The cliff path above provides some of the best shore-based whale watching in South Africa.

## 5. Pringlebaai Strand, Pringle Bay

Pringle Bay is a small village community about 75 kilometres southeast of Cape Town at the base of the Kogelberg Mountains. Pringlebaai Strand is a Blue Flag beach on the warmer Indian Ocean side of the Cape coast, protected from the southwest swells that hit the False Bay coast by the Hangklip headland. The beach is narrow but clean, and the village has developed into a popular weekend destination for Cape Town residents. The Kogelberg Biosphere Reserve inland is one of the most biodiverse areas in the Cape Floristic Region.

## 6. Silversands Beach, Gordon's Bay

Gordon's Bay sits at the eastern end of False Bay and benefits from the warmer water temperatures of the Indian Ocean system. Silversands is the main beach — wide, sandy, and well served by a promenade of restaurants and facilities. It is popular with Cape Town families for weekend trips and draws significant numbers from the Stellenbosch wine region nearby. The water is warm enough for genuine swimming from November through March. False Bay is also a prime shark research area, with great white sharks present in the bay during certain seasons.

## 7. Castle Beach, Gordons Bay Area

Castle Beach is a smaller beach south of the main Silversands strip in Gordon's Bay, generally quieter and more sheltered. It takes its name from the rocky outcrops that flank it. The beach is popular with local families and has basic facilities. The surrounding area has the characteristic character of the False Bay shoreline — low fynbos vegetation, mountain backdrop, and clear water relative to the industrial areas further north in the bay.

## 8. Kammabaai Beach, Gansbaai

Gansbaai, roughly 170 kilometres from Cape Town, is famous internationally as the great white shark capital of the world, as the narrow channel between Dyer Island and Geyser Rock — known as Shark Alley — concentrates large numbers of great whites feeding on the Cape fur seal colony. Kammabaai Beach is the local swimming beach at the edge of this area. The juxtaposition of a pleasant family beach town with the world's most intense great white shark concentration makes Gansbaai one of the Cape coast's most distinctive destinations.

## 9. Shipwreck Beach, Near Cape Point

Shipwreck Beach, on the eastern False Bay coast of the Cape Peninsula National Park south of Simonstown, is accessible only by a walking trail and takes its name from the corroded remnants of the Nolloth, a sailing vessel that ran aground in the mid-20th century. The beach is remote by Cape standards, entirely undeveloped, and backed by the fynbos and rocky scrub typical of the Table Mountain National Park. African penguins are found on this section of coast.

## 10. Sunset Rocks Beach, Kommetjie

Kommetjie is a small coastal village on the Atlantic side of the Peninsula, south of Cape Town, where the cold Benguela Current drives powerful swells onto a rocky coastline. Sunset Rocks beach is famous in South African surfing culture — the reef break here is one of the heaviest in the country when big south swells arrive, and Long Beach immediately north is the premier surf spot on the Peninsula. The coastline here is raw, windswept, and cold by any standard, but the wave quality attracts surfers from across southern Africa.

## Plan the Cape Coastal Drive

The Cape coast beaches sit within a relatively compact geography, and a dedicated coastal drive from Cape Town south to Hermanus and east to Gansbaai can cover most of the major spots in two or three days. However, the contrast between Atlantic and Indian Ocean conditions is dramatic enough that choosing a beach requires understanding which side of the Peninsula you are on.
