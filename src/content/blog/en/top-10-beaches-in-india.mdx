India has over 7,500 kilometres of coastline across four distinct coastal regions: the west coast (Konkan, Goa, Karnataka, Kerala) facing the Arabian Sea; the east coast (Andhra, Odisha, Tamil Nadu) on the Bay of Bengal; and two offshore territories — the Andaman and Nicobar Islands in the Bay of Bengal, and Lakshadweep in the Arabian Sea. The quality, character, and accessibility of beaches vary enormously across this range.

The monsoon divides the beach season: the southwest monsoon affects the west coast from June through September; the northeast monsoon hits the east coast from October through December. The Andaman Islands have the most complex seasonal pattern; November through April is generally optimal.

## 1. Radhanagar Beach, Andaman Islands

Radhanagar on Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep) in the Andaman archipelago is consistently rated the best beach in Asia by international travel publications, and the accolade is not misplaced. The beach is a 2-kilometre arc of fine white sand behind a protected mangrove and forest area, with clear Andaman Sea water of a vivid blue-turquoise colour. The sunset from the western end is exceptional. The beach is managed within the Andaman Islands protected area framework with limited infrastructure. Access is by ferry from Port Blair.

## 2. Palolem Beach, South Goa

Palolem in South Goa is a horseshoe bay backed by coconut palms with a hilly headland on each side, calm and shallow at the north end of the bay. The beach has good hut and guesthouse accommodation behind it, excellent seafood restaurants, and a music scene that extends into the evening without the more abrasive nightlife character of North Goa's Baga and Calangute. The bay's geometry is well-suited to kayaking. Season is October through April.

## 3. Varkala Beach, Kerala

Varkala is a cliff beach — the beach sits below laterite cliffs on the Arabian Sea coast of Kerala, backed by a cliff-top promenade of restaurants and guesthouses. The combination of the red cliffs, the black and sand beach below, and the Arabian Sea surf creates a striking landscape. The Papanasam Beach section has religious significance and the town has an Ayurvedic treatment tradition. Varkala is accessible from Thiruvananthapuram (Trivandrum) by road or rail.

## 4. Havelock Island – Neil Island, Andaman Islands

Neil Island (Shaheed Dweep) is quieter than Havelock and has the Andaman beach quality without the relative development of Radhanagar. Bharatpur Beach on the north coast has calm, clear water good for snorkelling. Laxmanpur Beach on the southwest has good sunset views. Neil Island is a half-hour ferry from Havelock or 2 hours from Port Blair.

## 5. Agonda Beach, South Goa

Agonda is a quiet fishing village beach south of Palolem with olive ridley sea turtle nesting in season (November through March). The beach is long, the accommodation low-key, and the character more local than international compared to North Goa. The turtle nesting season brings monitoring by conservation volunteers.

## 6. Tarkarli Beach, Maharashtra

Tarkarli in the Malvan district of Maharashtra is a beach on the north Konkan coast with unusually clear water for mainland India — the combination of rocky seabed and limited freshwater runoff keeps clarity high. The Sindhudurg Fort, built by Shivaji in the 17th century on an island in the bay, is accessible by boat. Water sports including scuba diving and snorkelling are operated here.

## 7. Marari Beach, Kerala

Marari near Alleppey (Alappuzha) is a quiet beach town known for a specific style of beach tourism that combines a stay at a beach resort with access to Kerala's Kuttanad backwaters. The beach is broad and sandy with coconut palms and fishing canoes pulled up on the sand. The fishermen's village character behind the beach is authentic.

## 8. Kudle Beach, Gokarna, Karnataka

Gokarna in Karnataka is a Hindu pilgrimage town with five beaches accessible by walking trail from each other along the coast. Kudle Beach is the largest and most accessible of the quieter beaches south of the main Om Beach. The combination of the beach and the town's pilgrimage identity gives Gokarna a character quite different from Goa.

## 9. Om Beach, Gokarna, Karnataka

Om Beach is named for its shape — two curved bays forming the Om symbol when seen from above. The beach is accessible by road and boat from Gokarna. It is busier than Kudle or Half Moon Beach further south. The coastline walking trail connecting the Gokarna beaches is one of Karnataka's best half-day walks.

## 10. Nilaveli Beach, Trincomalee, Sri Lanka (proximate)

Nilaveli, technically in Sri Lanka, is included here for proximity planning: visitors to the Andaman Islands or southern Indian coasts frequently combine with Sri Lanka's northeast beaches, of which Nilaveli — long, white, and fronting the most biodiverse reef in Sri Lanka — is the outstanding example. Nilaveli Beach is in the database.

## Planning an Indian beach trip

India's beach geography requires more logistical planning than most destinations because the best beaches are far apart and the transport connections between them are not always direct. The Andaman Islands require a flight from Kolkata, Chennai, or Delhi; Lakshadweep requires a permit and boat or flight from Kochi; Goa is accessible from Mumbai by flight, rail, or overnight bus. Planning two different beach regions on a single trip typically requires a return to a hub city.

The winter season from November through February is the optimal period across most of India's beach coast. The northeast monsoon affects the east coast in October and November, making Goa and Kerala the practical choices then; from December onwards, both coasts are in their prime season simultaneously.

Water safety at Indian beaches varies significantly. Goa's main tourist beaches have lifeguard services; the Andaman beaches are managed with fewer formal safety systems. Rip currents are present at most ocean-facing beaches, and drownings at Goa beaches are a regular occurrence in the monsoon fringe months of May and October when tourists arrive but conditions are still rough. The flag system at Goa's patrolled beaches is genuine — follow it.

Food at the Indian coast is one of the distinctive pleasures. Kerala's fish curry and prawn dishes, Goa's vindaloo and fish recheado using local mackerel and kingfish, and the seafood thali available at local eateries in the Andaman Islands are each specific to their region and not replicated elsewhere in the country.

The Andaman Islands are underappreciated as a beach destination by international visitors who default to Southeast Asia. Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep) is accessible from Port Blair by 2-hour ferry and has white sand beaches facing clear water. Radhanagar Beach on Havelock is widely cited as one of the best beaches in Asia. The coral condition around the Andamans is significantly better than around most of India's mainland coast, and the turtle and dugong populations in the Andaman Sea are among the most intact in the northern Indian Ocean.
