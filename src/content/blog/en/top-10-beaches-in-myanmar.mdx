Myanmar has over 2,800 kilometres of coastline on the Bay of Bengal and Andaman Sea, divided between the Rakhine coast in the northwest and the Tanintharyi coast in the southeast. The Rakhine coast, including the famous Ngapali beach and the longer stretch of Ngwe Saung and Chaungtha, has been the primary destination for beach tourism in Myanmar for decades. The Tanintharyi coast further south includes the Myeik Archipelago — also called the Mergui Archipelago — a vast scattering of over 800 islands with minimal infrastructure and extraordinary natural beauty. Myanmar's beach tourism has been significantly affected by political events since the military coup of February 2021, but the natural character of the beaches themselves remains extraordinary.

## 1. Ngapali Beach, Rakhine State

Ngapali is Myanmar's most famous beach, a three-kilometre arc of white sand on the Bay of Bengal in Rakhine State, accessible by a short domestic flight from Yangon or Mandalay. The beach faces west into the Bay of Bengal and the sunsets here are remarkable: a flat horizon over open water, fishing boats returning in the late afternoon, the light warm and long. Ngapali has a cluster of beach hotels ranging from budget to boutique, and the local fishing village of Ngapali itself has fresh seafood restaurants directly on the beach. The name is said to derive from the Rakhine pronunciation of Naples — Italian engineers worked in the area in the colonial period.

## 2. Maungmagan Beach, Tanintharyi Region

Maungmagan sits near the town of Dawei on the southern Tanintharyi coast, one of the few developed beaches in this long, largely undeveloped coastline. The beach is long and sandy, backed by coconut palms, with the warm, clear water of the Andaman Sea. Dawei itself is a significant town with a domestic airport, and Maungmagan is primarily used by Burmese domestic tourists rather than international visitors. The southern Tanintharyi coast has been the subject of large-scale industrial development plans — a proposed deep-sea port and Special Economic Zone at Dawei has been discussed for decades — but the beach area remains relatively intact.

## 3. Nabule Beach, Mon State

Nabule Beach sits in Mon State near the town of Ye, in the southernmost part of mainland Myanmar's Mon region. The beach is backed by forested hills and sees very few foreign visitors, functioning primarily as a local beach for communities in the Ye area. Mon State has a distinct cultural identity — the Mon people have their own language and traditions separate from the Burmese majority — and the beaches here exist within a coastal landscape that retains a genuinely undeveloped character. Access from Mawlamyine, Mon State's capital, takes several hours by road.

## 4. Ngwe Saung Beach, Ayeyarwady Region

Ngwe Saung — Silver Beach — is the second-longest beach in Myanmar after Chaungtha, stretching about 20 kilometres on the Ayeyarwady Region coast. The beach is accessible from Yangon by road (about five to six hours) or by an occasional speedboat service. Ngwe Saung has been developing gradually as a domestic resort beach, with a cluster of hotels ranging from simple guesthouses to mid-range beach resorts. The beach faces west into the Bay of Bengal and is good for swimming and beachcombing; the southern end near the fishing village retains a working character. Whale sharks have occasionally been sighted in the offshore waters.

## 5. Chaungtha Beach, Ayeyarwady Region

Chaungtha is the most popular beach resort on Myanmar's western coast for domestic Burmese tourists, a wide sandy beach about 180 kilometres from Yangon. The beach has developed a resort strip with hotels, restaurants, and watersports, predominantly oriented toward the domestic market. Chaungtha is one of the beaches with the most established infrastructure on the Ayeyarwady coast, and the combination of relative accessibility from Yangon and the broad, sandy beach makes it a genuine weekend destination for the capital's population. The sea here is warm and the waves moderate, suitable for swimming. [Open the map](/map) to see Chaungtha's position on the Ayeyarwady coast.

## 6. Setse Beach, Mon State

Setse Beach sits near the Kyaikkami area of Mon State, close to the famous Kyaikkami pagoda that appears to float on water at high tide. The beach is backed by shallow mangrove estuaries and the visual juxtaposition of the pagoda on the water and the open bay beyond it is one of the distinctive coastal images of southern Myanmar. The beach itself is sandy and the water warm, and the combination of the religious site and the coast gives the area a particular atmosphere. Kyaikkami is accessible from Mawlamyine by road in about two hours.

## 7. Teyzit Beach, Tanintharyi Region

Teyzit Beach lies in the Tanintharyi Region south of Myeik, in the area where the Tanintharyi coast begins to fragment into the hundreds of islands and channels of the Myeik Archipelago. The area has seen minimal development and access is difficult, requiring either a domestic flight to Myeik followed by boat transport, or a long road journey from Dawei. The reward is a coastline of extraordinary natural quality: white sand bays, clear Andaman Sea water, and dense forest backing the shore. The Myeik Archipelago was largely closed to foreigners until the 1990s and the infrastructure for independent travel remains limited.

## 8. Sin Htauk Beach, Tanintharyi Region

Sin Htauk is a beach in the greater Myeik area of the Tanintharyi Region, accessible by boat from the town of Myeik. Myeik is an old trading port — the Portuguese knew it as Mergui in the 16th century, and the town retains some colonial character in its older streets. The offshore islands around Myeik, including the area around Sin Htauk, have some of the clearest water in the Bay of Bengal and reef systems that are largely intact due to the area's limited accessibility. Live-aboard dive operators work out of Myeik and Thailand's Ranong to explore the Myeik Archipelago's diving.

## 9. Mayingyi Beach, Ayeyarwady Region

Mayingyi Beach sits in the Pathein area of the Ayeyarwady Region, on the Ayeyarwady Delta coast. The Ayeyarwady Delta is one of the largest river deltas in Asia, a landscape of channels, rice paddies, and fishing communities where the beach character is shaped by the rivers' sediment loads rather than by clear oceanic water. The beaches here are different from the clear-water Rakhine and Tanintharyi beaches: broader, with darker sand, and backed by the flat, intensively farmed landscape of the delta. Pathein is a significant city known for its handmade umbrellas — a traditional craft of the region.

## 10. Lon Wa Beach, Rakhine State

Lon Wa Beach sits in the Rakhine State coast south of Ngapali, in a region that is less developed than the Ngapali area. The Rakhine coast south of Ngapali has a number of beaches that see minimal international tourism, and Lon Wa is representative of the undeveloped character of this coastline: white sand, clear Bay of Bengal water, fishing communities, and the dense vegetation of the Rakhine coast behind. Rakhine State's access has been affected by political and humanitarian issues — the Rohingya crisis of 2017 significantly disrupted the region — and visitors planning travel here should check current conditions carefully.

Myanmar's beaches reward careful geographic planning, with the central Rakhine coast most accessible and the southern Tanintharyi and Myeik Archipelago requiring more logistics.
