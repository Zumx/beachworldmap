The Andaman and Nicobar Islands form an archipelago of more than 500 islands stretching roughly 800 kilometres across the northeastern Bay of Bengal, closer to Myanmar and Thailand than to the Indian mainland. Only a few dozen of the islands are inhabited, and fewer still are open to foreign visitors. The administrative capital Port Blair on South Andaman Island is the entry point for almost all visitors, accessible by daily flights from Chennai, Kolkata, and Delhi or by a roughly three-day ferry journey. The islands sit above one of the world's most intact coral reef systems in the Indian Ocean region, and their beaches benefit from extremely low industrial activity in the surrounding waters. The best season is November through April, when seas are calm and visibility underwater is typically excellent. ## 1. Radhanagar Beach, Havelock Island

Radhanagar is consistently ranked among the best beaches in Asia and has appeared on numerous international best-beach lists since the early 2000s. It occupies Beach No. 7 on the western side of Havelock Island (officially renamed Swaraj Dweep), and its combination of powder-white sand, turquoise water, and dense forest backing is genuinely exceptional. The beach faces west and the sunsets are spectacular. Swimming is safe in the shallower sections, though the beach drops off reasonably quickly. The 2.5-kilometre crescent sees moderate tourism volume by most standards, but the distance from the mainland keeps it less crowded than comparable beaches in Thailand or the Maldives.

## 2. Elephant Beach, Havelock Island

On the northeastern coast of Havelock, Elephant Beach is the primary snorkelling destination in the Andamans and accessible by a 30-minute boat ride from the jetty or a 45-minute forest trail. The shallow reef immediately offshore is one of the more accessible in the archipelago and supports significant coral cover and reef fish diversity including groupers, parrotfish, and moray eels. The beach itself is small and narrow at high tide, but the underwater topography is the main attraction. Most visitors combine a few hours here with a boat day trip.

## 3. Havelock Beach (Vijaynagar), Havelock Island

Beach No. 5 on Havelock, also called Vijaynagar Beach, is the longest stretch on the island and the closest to the main settlement. It is primarily used by local residents and is less touristed than Radhanagar. The beach runs for over 2 kilometres through alternating sandy stretches and rocky outcrops, and the forest comes down close to the shore at its northern end. Sea turtles nest here during November and December. The swimming conditions are generally good in the central section.

## 4. Neil Island Beach (Bharatpur), Neil Island

Neil Island (officially Shaheed Dweep) is a day trip or short overnight stop from Havelock, and Bharatpur Beach on the northeastern shore is its most visited spot. The shallow bay here protects a natural swimming and snorkelling area with moderate coral cover, and glass-bottom boat rides are available for those who prefer not to get wet. The island produces a significant proportion of Andaman vegetables and has a rural, unhurried character very different from Havelock.

## 5. Corbyn's Cove, Port Blair

The nearest beach to Port Blair town, Corbyn's Cove is a horseshoe bay about 7 kilometres south of the city centre, backed by coconut palms and accessible by road. It is the most convenient swimming beach for visitors staying in Port Blair, and while it lacks the spectacular remoteness of the outer island beaches, the water is clean and there is basic infrastructure including changing rooms, food stalls, and jet ski hire. It serves as a useful orientation point before island-hopping.

## 6. Serenity Beach, South Andaman

On the southern end of South Andaman Island, Serenity Beach is a less frequently visited stretch that rewards the 15-kilometre drive south from Port Blair. The beach is wide and relatively undeveloped, with casuarina trees providing shade. It is a popular spot for picnics among Port Blair residents and sees far fewer tourist visitors than Corbyn's Cove. The swimming here is safe during the calm season.

## 7. Promenade Beach, Port Blair

The Promenade waterfront in Port Blair is more of an esplanade than a swimming beach, but it functions as the social gathering point of the capital and provides views across the harbour to the historic Cellular Jail. The Promenade is the place to watch the daily ferry traffic between islands, observe the large fishing fleet at anchor, and understand Port Blair as a functioning port town rather than simply a transit hub. A small beach section at the southern end is used for wading.

## 8. Eden Beach, Baratang Island

Baratang Island lies roughly halfway between Port Blair and North Andaman and is most famous for its mud volcanoes and limestone caves — accessible through the forest reserve that crosses the Great Andaman Trunk Road. Eden Beach on the western coast is a remote, rarely visited stretch that requires a boat from the Baratang jetty. The lack of infrastructure is both the challenge and the attraction; the beach is genuinely undisturbed and the water is typically clear.

## 9. Baby Beach, Near Port Blair

Baby Beach is a small, very calm bay near the village of Wandoor, about 25 kilometres south of Port Blair and adjacent to the Mahatma Gandhi Marine National Park. It is primarily known as a snorkelling and glass-bottom boat launch point for the national park, which encompasses some of the best-protected coral in the southern Andamans. Visitor numbers to the national park are managed, and the beach itself sees moderate traffic on weekends from Port Blair day-trippers.

## 10. Sanguthurai Beach, North Andaman

Sanguthurai is a remote beach on the northwestern coast of North Andaman Island near Diglipur, the northernmost significant settlement in the archipelago accessible to visitors. This part of the Andamans sees very few international tourists and the beach, backed by dense tropical forest, is typically empty. Turtle Bay near Diglipur is the primary sea turtle nesting site in the northern Andamans. Saddle Peak, the highest point in the entire archipelago at 732 metres, is visible from the coastline here.

## Plan Your Andaman Route

The Andaman Islands require deliberate planning — inter-island ferries run on fixed schedules and accommodation is limited on the outer islands.
