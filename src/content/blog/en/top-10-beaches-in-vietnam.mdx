Vietnam has 3,260 kilometres of coastline on the South China Sea, the Gulf of Thailand, and the Gulf of Tonkin. The geography runs north to south through multiple climate zones, which means the beach season varies significantly by region. The central coast (Da Nang, Hoi An) is best from March through August. The south coast (Mui Ne, Vung Tau) is best from November through April. Phu Quoc island in the Gulf of Thailand has its best weather from November through April. The far north (Ha Long Bay) is best from October through April.

The combination of a long coastline, diverse geography, and a well-developed domestic and international tourism infrastructure makes Vietnam one of Asia's most practical and rewarding beach destinations.

## 1. Sao Beach, Phu Quoc Island

Sao Beach (Bai Sao) on the southern tip of Phu Quoc island is the best beach on the island and one of the best in Vietnam: fine white sand, clear water, and a view of the An Thoi archipelago's limestone islands offshore. Phu Quoc is in the Gulf of Thailand — the water is protected and warm year-round in season. The island has developed substantially since 2010, but Sao Beach retains a better condition than the north coast resort strip.

## 2. Long Beach (Bai Truong), Phu Quoc

Long Beach on Phu Quoc's west coast is the island's main tourist beach — a 20-kilometre straight beach facing the Gulf of Thailand sunset. The resort development behind it ranges from budget guesthouses to international chains. The beach faces west and the sunsets are good. The water is calmer on the west coast than the east. Long Beach is the practical base for most Phu Quoc visits.

## 3. Mui Ne Beach, Binh Thuan Province

Mui Ne on the south-central coast is known for three things: the kitesurfing (it is Southeast Asia's most consistent kite destination, receiving the northeast monsoon strongly from November through March), the red sand dunes behind the beach, and the famous fishing village of Mui Ne itself (a crescent of fishing boats in a sheltered bay). The beach strip along the tourist road from Ham Tien village to Mui Ne cape has the full resort infrastructure. Bai tắm Ham Tien area in the database reflects this.

## 4. An Bang Beach, Hoi An, Quang Nam

An Bang, 3 kilometres from Hoi An's ancient town, is the closest beach to one of Vietnam's most visited cultural destinations. The beach is long, the sand pale, and the water warmest from June through August. The combination of the Hoi An tailoring and cooking school circuit with An Bang beach makes it a complete destination. Cua Dai Beach, nearby, was once more famous but has suffered severe erosion.

## 5. My Khe Beach, Da Nang

My Khe is Da Nang's main city beach — a broad, flat, well-maintained strip facing the South China Sea. My Khe is in the database. The beach has a full lifeguard and cleaning infrastructure, is wide enough to absorb the significant Da Nang summer crowd, and the city's restaurant culture is accessible behind it. The Marble Mountains — a cluster of limestone hills with pagodas and caves — are 8 kilometres south.

## 6. Lang Co Beach, Hue Province

Lang Co is a 10-kilometre sand spit between the sea and a large lagoon, on the coastal pass between Da Nang and Hue (the Hai Van Pass). The beach is largely undeveloped — a few small hotels and the InterContinental Danang nearby — and the combination of beach, lagoon, and the mountain pass above make it one of Vietnam's most scenic coastal locations. The road over the Hai Van Pass (which can be biked) adds to the experience.

## 7. Ninh Van Bay, Khanh Hoa Province

Ninh Van Bay near Nha Trang is accessible only by boat (15 minutes from the Ninh Vân pier). The bay has several small beaches in enclosed coves; the Six Senses Ninh Van Bay resort operates on one section. The white sand coves and granite rock backdrop are specific to this bay.

## 8. Nha Trang Beach, Khanh Hoa Province

Nha Trang is Vietnam's most developed beach resort city — a 6-kilometre urban beach in a bay with offshore islands and a full infrastructure of hotels, restaurants, and water sports. Nha Trang Beach is in the database. The beach has a municipal improvement programme and consistent Blue Flag-equivalent status by Vietnamese standards. The Hon Mun Marine Protected Area offshore has the best diving in southern Vietnam.

## 9. Bai Xep Beach, Phu Yen Province

Bai Xep, a small beach in the fishing village of Phu Yen Province north of Quy Nhon, was featured in the film Pan (2015) and has attracted a small number of boutique accommodation operations. The beach is backed by a hillside of casuarina trees and faces the South China Sea. Quy Nhon nearby has expanded its tourism infrastructure but remains less busy than Da Nang or Nha Trang.

## 10. Vung Tau Beach, Ba Ria-Vung Tau Province

Vung Tau is the beach escape from Ho Chi Minh City — 2 hours by road or 1 hour by hydrofoil from Saigon. The beach city has both a front beach (Bai Truoc, facing the bay) and a back beach (Bai Sau, facing the open sea). The back beach is longer and better for swimming. Vung Tau is primarily a domestic and expat weekend destination; its infrastructure reflects this: functional rather than glamorous.

## Planning Vietnam's beaches

Vietnam's beach geography rewards understanding the north-south climate gradient before booking. The central and southern coasts are affected by two distinct monsoon systems, which means that while one region has its best beach weather, another is in its wet season. The practical result is that Vietnam beach destinations are good in rotation throughout most of the year — but not all regions simultaneously.

November through April is the best window for the south: Phu Quoc, Mui Ne, Vung Tau. The northeast monsoon drives reliable dry weather to the Gulf of Thailand coast. Phu Quoc's west coast is calm; Mui Ne's northeast monsoon simultaneously delivers the kiting wind that makes it Southeast Asia's best kitesurfing destination.

March through August is the best window for the central coast: Da Nang, Hoi An, My Khe, Lang Co. The central region's rain shadow (the Annamite Range blocks weather from the west) and the summer position of the Intertropical Convergence Zone keep this section dry and sunny.

Da Nang has become one of Southeast Asia's most accessible beach cities: direct flights from numerous Asian hubs, a manageable city centre with good restaurant infrastructure, and My Khe Beach within 20 minutes of the airport. The combination of Hoi An's cultural heritage (a UNESCO World Heritage ancient town 30 kilometres south) and An Bang beach makes Da Nang one of the best multi-day beach destinations in Asia for visitors who want more than just sand.

Phu Quoc has developed rapidly as Vietnam's most international beach destination: a modern international airport with direct connections from Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, and regional Asian hubs, resort infrastructure from budget guesthouses to international luxury chains, and the best beach quality in Vietnam at Sao Beach on the south tip. The northern part of the island retains a quieter character. The An Thoi archipelago offshore from Sao Beach is accessible by boat for snorkelling; the coral is modest by Southeast Asian standards but improving under marine protection management.
