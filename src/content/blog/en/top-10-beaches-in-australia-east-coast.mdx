Australia's east coast runs from Cape York in the tropical north to the cold Southern Ocean shores of Victoria and New South Wales, a coastline of over 6,000 kilometres. The beach character changes markedly from north to south: tropical northern Queensland has the Coral Sea and the Great Barrier Reef fringing a coast of estuaries and mangroves; the mid-Queensland and New South Wales coast delivers the classic Australian surf beach — wide sand, headland-separated bays, consistent south Pacific swell; Victoria and southern NSW brings cold water, rugged Southern Ocean conditions, and dramatic cliff scenery. The beaches below span this range.

## 1. Whitehaven Beach, Whitsunday Island, Queensland

Whitehaven is consistently ranked the best beach in Australia and among the best in the world. The 7-kilometre beach is composed of 98 percent pure silica sand — so fine it squeaks underfoot and does not heat up in the sun the way dark mineral sands do. The colour at Hill Inlet, where tidal movement swirls the white sand against teal and turquoise water, is extraordinary. Access is by boat or seaplane from Airlie Beach. There are no permanent residents on Whitsunday Island. The beach is in the database.

## 2. Bondi Beach, New South Wales

Bondi is Sydney's iconic beach and the spiritual home of Australian surf culture. The 1-kilometre crescent faces south into the Tasman Sea. The surf ranges from flat to overhead depending on swell. The Bondi to Coogee coastal walk from the south end of the beach is one of Sydney's best half-day activities. The Bondi Surf Bathers' Life Saving Club, founded in 1906, is the oldest in the world. Bondi Beach is in the database. [Read the deep dive on Bondi.](/blog/en/bondi-beach-deep-dive)

## 3. Manly Beach, New South Wales

Manly is on the northern beaches of Sydney, reached by ferry from Circular Quay in 30 minutes across the harbour — one of the world's great commuter rides. The beach faces northeast and receives consistent Pacific swell. The promenade of Norfolk Island pines, the pedestrian corso connecting the ocean beach to the harbour, and the village character of the suburb make it functionally different from Bondi. Manly Beach is in the database.

## 4. Byron Bay, New South Wales

Byron Bay is the most easterly point of mainland Australia, a beach town that became a counterculture and surf destination in the 1970s and has since developed into one of the country's most visited coastal towns. The main beach faces northeast with Watego's Beach (a right-hand point break) at the cape. Main Beach in town is surf-patrolled and broad. The Cape Byron Lighthouse, the easternmost point, can be reached on foot from the beach. The town has significant food and accommodation infrastructure.

## 5. Fraser Island (K'gari), Queensland

K'gari is the world's largest sand island, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and one of Australia's most extraordinary natural areas. Seventy-Five Mile Beach on the island's eastern coast is literally a highway — aircraft land on it and four-wheel drives drive its length. The beach faces the open Pacific and has no swimming (Portuguese man o' war are common and the surf is powerful), but it is the access route to the island's freshwater lakes, including Lake McKenzie — a perched freshwater lake over white sand with brilliant clear water.

## 6. Noosa, Sunshine Coast, Queensland

Noosa Main Beach, within the Noosa National Park, is a north-facing beach sheltered from south swell and backed by a national park headland. The calm conditions make it a reliable swimming beach. The headland walking track from Noosa Main Beach is one of Queensland's best coastal walks, with several smaller beaches — Tea Tree Bay, Granite Bay — accessible on foot. Noosa town has a high density of good restaurants for a coastal resort.

## 7. Hyams Beach, Jervis Bay, New South Wales

Hyams Beach holds a Guinness World Record claim for the whitest sand in the world. The beach is on the western shore of Jervis Bay, about 2.5 hours south of Sydney. Jervis Bay's geography — a large, sheltered bay with no significant river inflow — keeps the water exceptionally clear. The sand is pure white quartz. The bay has dolphins year-round and whale migration passes close offshore in October and November. The beach is small and popular on weekends; weekday visits are calmer.

## 8. Bells Beach, Torquay, Victoria

Bells Beach is Australia's most famous surf beach and the home of the Rip Curl Pro — the world's longest-running professional surfing contest, held here each Easter. The right-hand point break over a rocky reef delivers powerful waves suited to advanced surfers. The clifftop viewing platform above the break gives non-surfers a grandstand view. Bells is 100 kilometres southwest of Melbourne and is accessible as a day trip.

## 9. Lucky Bay, Cape Le Grand National Park, Western Australia

Lucky Bay is technically on the south coast of Western Australia and covered separately in the west coast list, but its fame warrants mention here too — kangaroos lie on the beach routinely, the sand is white quartzite, and the Southern Ocean water is a vivid turquoise. Lucky Bay is in the database.

## 10. Mission Beach, Queensland

Mission Beach is the closest mainland beach to the Great Barrier Reef — Dunk Island is visible offshore. The beach is long, dark-sanded, and backed by World Heritage rainforest. Cassowaries walk through the gardens of nearby properties. This is where the Wet Tropics meets the ocean — the Cassowary Coast character is specific to this stretch of Queensland and unlike anywhere else on the east coast.

## Planning the east coast

The Great Barrier Reef season runs year-round but the stinger (box jellyfish) season in tropical Queensland runs from October through May — swimming in tropical Queensland waters without a stinger suit during this period is not recommended. Outside this season, tropical Queensland beaches are among the best in Australia; the Whitsundays, Mission Beach, and the Cape Tribulation coast have warm water and no stinger risk from June through September.

The east coast from Brisbane to Cairns is a classic gap-year and backpacker circuit, well-served by bus passes (Greyhound Australia and Premier Motor Service) that allow travel between any combination of stops. The circuit takes a minimum of 2 weeks to do justice; 4 to 6 weeks allows a thorough exploration including time at key beaches (Byron Bay, the Whitsundays, Cape Tribulation), the Great Barrier Reef, and the interior (Carnarvon Gorge, the outback hinterland). The Whitsundays specifically require at least 2 days for a boat trip that includes Whitehaven Beach — day trip boats do the 7-kilometre beach; overnight sailing allows Hill Inlet views at optimal tide and the ability to be on the beach in the early morning before day-trip arrivals.

The Sydney beaches — Bondi, Manly, Cronulla, the northern beaches stretching to Palm Beach — are covered more thoroughly in the Bondi Beach deep dive. Sydney's beach geography is one of the city's defining assets: 70 beaches within the greater Sydney area, served by ferries, buses, and train connections that make beach access feasible without a car.

## Surf lifesaving and the flag system

Every patrolled beach in Queensland and New South Wales operates the red-and-yellow flag system introduced by the Bondi Surf Bathers' Life Saving Club in 1906. The flags mark the zone patrolled by lifeguards and positioned specifically to avoid the main rip currents that form and move position with tide and swell changes. Swimming between the flags is the single most important beach safety behaviour on Australian beaches.

In Queensland, Surf Life Saving Queensland operates over 60 patrol areas covering most major beaches. In New South Wales, Surf Life Saving NSW covers a similar number. Most major beaches are patrolled year-round on weekends and public holidays, and daily from roughly September through April. Checking the local club's schedule before visiting an unfamiliar beach is advisable, particularly for beaches outside the main tourist areas.
