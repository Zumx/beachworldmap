Most beach guides prioritise appearance: the whitest sand, the clearest water, the most photogenic scenery. For families with young children, the more useful criteria are different. How long can you actually stay in comfort? Is there natural shade from trees or cliffs? Is the water calm enough for non-swimmers? Are there toilets, a place to buy food, and somewhere to rinse sand off children before getting in the car?

The beaches below earn their place on a family list not primarily because of their beauty — though many are beautiful — but because they work well for the reality of a day at the beach with children.

## 1. Playa de Muro, Mallorca, Spain

Playa de Muro in the Bay of Alcúdia on Mallorca's north coast is 6 kilometres of fine white sand with a dedicated family infrastructure: public showers, toilets, playgrounds, a promenade behind the beach, and sunlounger hire with umbrellas. The water is calm and shallow for a very long way out, making it genuinely safe for young swimmers. Pine woodland behind parts of the beach provides natural shade if you walk 5 minutes from the main beach infrastructure. It is the reference point for Mediterranean family beaches for good reason.

## 2. Falmouth Beach, Cornwall, England

Falmouth in Cornwall has a calm town beach, Gyllyngvase, backed by a café and a grassy slope shaded by mature trees. The beach faces south, the water is sheltered, and the town behind has everything a family needs. The sand is fine and golden by Cornish standards. The Swanpool Beach a short walk to the west has a freshwater lake adjacent to the sea beach, popular with children. The climate is unpredictable but the infrastructure compensates: the surf schools, cafés, and nearby national trust coast paths mean a rainy morning can be easily pivoted.

## 3. Kata Beach, Phuket, Thailand

Of Phuket's beaches, Kata is the most suitable for families with young children during the dry season (November through April). The bay is enclosed enough to provide calmer conditions than Karon or Patong, the sand is wide and soft, and the beach has a full infrastructure of restaurants and shops behind the shoreline. The water is warm year-round. During the shoulder season there is more natural shade as the beach is less congested and the southern headland trees cast shadows on the southern end. During wet season (May through October), Kata Beach is unsuitable for swimming.

## 4. Playa de la Concha, San Sebastián, Spain

Playa de la Concha in San Sebastián is a sheltered urban beach in a horseshoe bay with calm water, a promenade with shade trees, a municipal swimming pool attached to the beach, and the full café-restaurant infrastructure of a city beach. The bay's geometry and the offshore island of Santa Clara reduce wave action significantly. The Basque coast's weather is more temperate than Andalucia's, which means midday shade matters less for UV but more for comfort in the warmest months.

## 5. Hammamet Beach, Tunisia

Hammamet on Tunisia's Gulf of Hammamet coast is one of the Mediterranean's major family resort destinations. The beach is long and sandy with a warm, shallow sea that shelves very gradually. The resort hotels behind the beach provide umbrellas and sunlounger service for guests; public beach sections also have umbrella hire. The town's medina and jasmine market are within walking distance for afternoon culture. August is the peak family month; May and September offer better prices and less heat.

## 6. Kuta Beach, Bali, Indonesia

Kuta Beach near Denpasar is one of the few internationally known Bali beaches where the surf conditions are suitable for beginner-level swimming lessons and family visits during the May through September dry season. Surfing schools run beginner classes directly on the beach. The beach has a wide flat sand section above the surf zone where shade from portable umbrellas and beach vendors is abundant. It is busy, commercial, and not pristine, but the infrastructure — toilets, food, nearby facilities, and the gentle learning waves — makes it functional for families.

## 7. Plage du Prado, Marseille, France

Plage du Prado is an artificial beach constructed on the Mediterranean coast of Marseille using sand brought in during the 1980s. The beach is large, managed, patrolled by lifeguards in season, and backed by a parkland with trees providing genuine shade. The geometry of the bay provides relatively calm water. For a city beach accessible without a car or long transfer, it is highly efficient, and the proximity of the Calanques National Park to the south means older children and teens have excellent hiking and snorkelling options within a short bus ride.

## 8. Lanikai Beach, Oahu, Hawaii, USA

Lanikai on Oahu's Windward coast is a short, narrow residential beach with exceptionally calm water behind an offshore reef. The sheltered conditions make it ideal for young swimmers. There is no commercial infrastructure on the beach itself — no lifeguards, no toilets — but the residential neighbourhood has public access points. The water is so calm and clear that young children can see the reef fish below them from standing depth. The offshore Mokulua islands are visible from the beach and provide a visual goal for confident junior swimmers.

## 9. Copacabana Beach, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

Copacabana Beach in Rio is not an obvious choice for a family shade listing, but the beach is genuinely family-functional. The beach's width — 50 to 80 metres of sand — means that settling up near the back of the beach gives late afternoon shade from the apartment buildings behind. The safe swimming zone inside the flags is well-monitored by lifeguards. The beach vendors provide food, cold drinks, and sun equipment directly to the sand. The Copacabana neighbourhood behind has all logistics: supermarkets, pharmacies, and accommodation at multiple price points. For families that can handle an urban Brazilian beach environment, the infrastructure is exceptional.

## 10. Batu Ferringhi, Penang, Malaysia

Batu Ferringhi on the northwest coast of Penang island is a long beach backed by mature trees and a string of large resort hotels. The shade from the trees at the rear of the beach provides a genuine retreat from midday sun, and the beach has good facilities. The water is warm year-round and the wave action is mild. The night market at Batu Ferringhi in the evening is an appealing family activity within walking distance of all the beach hotels. Penang's food culture — the best hawker food in Malaysia — is accessible throughout the island. Pantai Batu Feringgi is in the map database.

## Planning for shade

Natural shade from palms, casuarina trees, or headland cliffs is always cooler than shade from a beach umbrella in still conditions. When inspecting a beach for a family visit, check the orientation relative to the sun's arc for the time of day you will be there: a beach facing west that is shaded by morning cliffs may be in full sun from noon onwards. A beach backed by pine trees provides shade from early afternoon.

A practical structure for families in high UV destinations: arrive at the beach early (8 to 9am), use the first 2 to 3 hours productively in the water and on the sand, move to a shaded restaurant or interior activity for the 11am to 3pm window, and return to the beach in the late afternoon (3 to 5pm) when UV levels are falling but the sea is still warm. This 3-hour midday break significantly reduces UV exposure and the cumulative fatigue that hot beach days produce in children — and parents.
