Clothing-optional and naturist beaches exist on every continent with a coastline and in legal frameworks ranging from formally designated public nudist zones to tolerant informality on remote beaches. The culture and expectations differ significantly between destinations. Understanding the local norm — and the difference between a managed naturist beach and an informal nude beach — makes the experience more straightforward for newcomers.

## 1. Hanlan's Point, Toronto Island, Canada

Hanlan's Point on Toronto Island in Lake Ontario is one of the most surprising clothing-optional beaches in the world by location: a federally designated nude beach within a 15-minute ferry ride of a major North American city. The designation dates from a 2000 by-law. The beach is long and sandy, the lake water swimmable in summer, and the naturist community that uses the beach is well-established and operates by the standard naturist etiquette of respectful behaviour and no photography of others. Ferries from the Jack Layton Ferry Terminal run regularly. The beach is well maintained and family-attended.

## 2. Cap d'Agde, France

Cap d'Agde in the Hérault department of southern France is Europe's largest naturist resort village, with accommodation, restaurants, shopping, a marina, and kilometres of beach enclosed within a dedicated naturist zone. Entry requires paying an access fee. The scale is genuinely town-sized: several thousand people live in the naturist village in summer. The beach is on the Mediterranean and the water is calm and warm in season. Cap d'Agde attracts a broad demographic and has an established infrastructure that removes the ambiguity of informal nude beaches.

## 3. Playa de ses Illetes, Formentera, Spain

Formentera, the smallest of the Balearic Islands, is reached by ferry from Ibiza. Playa de ses Illetes is a long, narrow spit of sand with crystalline shallow water on both sides — considered by many the most beautiful beach in the western Mediterranean. Nudism is widespread here and accepted on most sections of the beach. The sand is white, the water is an impossible turquoise, and the beach remains less overdeveloped than comparable Ibiza beaches. Day trips from Ibiza are possible; staying on Formentera requires booking well ahead in summer.

## 4. Wreck Beach, Vancouver, Canada

Wreck Beach at the University of British Columbia in Vancouver is a 7-kilometre clothing-optional beach beneath cliffs accessed by a steep trail down from the university campus. It is one of North America's most visited naturist beaches, informal in character and legally protected under a university bylaw. Vendors walk the beach in summer. The setting — backed by old-growth forest with a view of the Strait of Georgia and Vancouver Island — is unlike any other naturist beach in the world.

## 5. Playa de la Cala de ses Illes, Ibiza, Spain

Ibiza has multiple informal nude beaches, of which the coves around the north and east coasts are the most established. Cala Xarraca and the smaller coves near Portinatx are frequently cited. The Balearic Island tradition of toplessness across most beaches shades into full nudism in certain bays without formal designation, and this is widely accepted.

## 6. Little Beach, Maui, Hawaii, USA

Little Beach (Pu'u Olu'ai) on Maui's south shore is technically illegal for nudity under Hawaii state law but has been used as an unofficial nude beach for decades. On Sunday evenings the beach hosts a drum circle that attracts both clothed and unclothed beachgoers. The beach is accessed by climbing over a lava rock point from Big Beach (Makena Beach State Park). The setting is dramatic — black lava, turquoise water, and Molokini visible offshore. The unofficial status means that conditions change; check current local advice before visiting.

## 7. Red Beach, Matala, Crete, Greece

Red Beach near Matala in southern Crete has been an informal nude beach since the hippie era of the 1960s and 1970s when Matala's cave dwellings attracted a counterculture community. The beach is red-tinged due to iron-rich volcanic rock, reached by a 15-minute walk over a headland from Matala. Nudism continues here without official designation and is generally undisturbed. The beach has no facilities; bring everything you need.

## 8. Samurai Beach, NSW, Australia

Australia has multiple clothing-optional beaches. Samurai Beach in New South Wales is a designated naturist beach managed by the Australian Naturist Federation, with a calm surf break, good facilities, and a long-established community. It is north of Port Stephens, about three hours from Sydney. Australia's naturist beach network is well-distributed along the eastern and southern coasts; the Australian Naturist Federation maintains a current list.

## 9. Playa de los Muertos, Almería, Spain

Playa de los Muertos near Carboneras in Almería is an isolated pebble-and-sand beach accessible by a steep track from the clifftop. It is one of the clearest water beaches on the Andalusian coast, backed by the Cabo de Gata-Níjar Natural Park. Nudism is practised here informally and widely. No facilities — no shade, no vendors, no toilets. The clarity of the water and the dramatic cliffs make it worth the access challenge.

## 10. Praia de Naturista, Tambaba, Brazil

Tambaba in Paraíba state, northeast Brazil, is the only official naturist beach in northeastern Brazil — a federal designation providing a defined naturist zone. The beach is accessed through a gate with posted rules. The Atlantic water is warm year-round. Local naturist clubs manage the infrastructure. Tambaba represents the more organised end of Brazilian naturist beach culture, which also has informal nude beach traditions at remote access points along the northeastern coast.

## Etiquette at clothing-optional beaches

Behaviour norms at naturist and clothing-optional beaches are more consistent than newcomers expect. Photography of other beach users without explicit consent is universally considered unacceptable and is aggressively policed at managed beaches. Staring is similarly unwelcome. The culture is broadly non-sexual and family-oriented at managed naturist facilities; the atmosphere at informal beaches varies. Sitting on a towel is standard practice. Bringing children is accepted at most naturist beaches and resorts.

## Legal status and the distinction from informal beaches

The legal frameworks for nudism vary significantly by country and location. Formally designated naturist beaches — with municipal or federal designation — operate with legal protection for naturist use. These include Wreck Beach in Vancouver, Hanlan's Point in Toronto, and designated naturist zones in France and Germany.

Informal nude beaches — places where nudism has been practised for decades by convention without formal designation — have no legal protection but are tolerated by local authorities in practice. These include most of the Mediterranean cove beaches where nudism is widespread, Red Beach at Matala, and Little Beach in Maui. The tolerance can change: new local administration, a specific incident, or a change in policing priorities can convert a long-established informal nude beach into an enforced clothed beach. Checking current local status before visiting an informal beach is advisable.

Deliberately using a non-designated clothed beach for nudism — ignoring the cultural or legal norms of that specific location — is not etiquette-neutral naturism. Naturist ethics are universally clear on this point: nudism is appropriate only where it is either formally designated or where the local convention clearly establishes it as acceptable.
