Israel's Mediterranean coastline is compact but remarkably varied. Running roughly 190 kilometres from the Lebanese border in the north to the Gaza fringe in the south, it encompasses ancient Phoenician harbours, Crusader-era sea walls, sandy ribbon beaches backed by banana plantations, and the cliff-edged coast of Mount Carmel. The northern stretch, from Tel Aviv northward through Netanya, Hadera, Haifa, Acre, and Nahariya, holds beaches shaped by centuries of commerce and conflict, each one carrying a story alongside the breaking waves. Most are free or low cost, well-maintained by local authorities, and reachable by train, making this one of the most accessible Mediterranean coastal regions anywhere. Summer runs May through October, water temperatures peak around 28°C in August, and the prevailing westerly breeze keeps the heat bearable on the sand.

## 1. Hof HaTzvaim (Turtle Beach), Sharon Coast

Hof HaTzvaim — literally Turtle Beach — sits between Netanya and Hadera on the Sharon Coast and earns its name from the loggerhead sea turtle nesting activity monitored by the Israel Nature and Parks Authority each summer. The beach is backed by low sandstone cliffs that shelter a relatively narrow strand from the full force of afternoon sea winds. Sand quality is fine and pale. Lifeguards staff the central section from June to September. Nesting season runs May through July, during which a portion of the upper beach is cordoned off; outside that zone swimming is unencumbered. The nearest rail connection is Netanya station, roughly 15 minutes by taxi.

## 2. Hof Gay (Pleasure Beach), Haifa Bay

Hof Gay occupies a flat stretch on the southern arc of Haifa Bay, backed by the industrial port cityscape yet offering clean, organised bathing that attracts Haifa families throughout the summer. Sand here is brown and medium-grained — not the whitest on the coast, but firm enough for beach sports. The bay's semi-enclosed geography calms the swell considerably compared to the open coast further south, making it suitable for young children and casual swimmers. Seasonal beach volleyball courts and snack kiosks operate from June onwards. The beach is reachable from Haifa's Bat Galim district, itself home to a historic promenade.

## 3. Hof Ganim (Park Beach), Northern Haifa

Hof Ganim is a community beach within the Ganim neighbourhood on the northern slopes of the Carmel ridge approaching the sea. Mature tamarisk trees provide shade at the land edge — unusual for Israeli beaches, which are typically wide-open. This tree cover makes late-afternoon visits in July and August considerably more comfortable than at exposed alternatives. A concrete promenade running alongside the sand connects to Bat Galim further south. Water visibility is generally good, the bottom is sandy to a depth of two metres, and the beach retains a local rather than tourist character. No entrance fee. Haifa's Light Rail network runs within walking distance.

## 4. Rimonim Beach, Nahariya

Rimonim Beach sits at the northern end of Nahariya, the last major city before Lebanon, roughly 35 kilometres north of Haifa. The beach takes its name from a hotel complex that anchors its southern end and provides changing facilities, showers, and a café. The strand here is wide and sandy, sheltered to the north by a small natural breakwater that creates calmer shallows ideal for families. Nahariya itself has a pleasant main boulevard lined with eucalyptus trees, and the combination of beach and old-fashioned Israeli resort town gives Rimonim a relaxed character that the larger city beaches lack. Train service from Tel Aviv takes about 90 minutes.

## 5. Hof Lido, Hadera

Hof Lido is one of the main organised beaches at Hadera, an industrial port city mid-way between Tel Aviv and Haifa. Despite the area's power station and desalination plant, the beach itself is clean and regularly monitored for water quality. Long breakwater jetties extending into the sea create artificial calm zones particularly useful for children and beginner swimmers. A seasonal facility with food stalls, sun-lounger hire, and lifeguard towers operates from late May. The Hadera train station is accessible by express from Tel Aviv in under 30 minutes, and local buses continue to the beach.

## 6. Hof HaTzuk (Cliff Beach), Northern Tel Aviv

Hof HaTzuk occupies the northernmost section of Tel Aviv's beach strip, set directly beneath the yellow sandstone cliff — tzuk in Hebrew — from which it takes its name. The cliff rises about 20 metres and provides dramatic visual framing, shelter from north-westerly winds, and a consistent backdrop for sunset photographs. The beach is wide, the sand white-yellow, and it sits at the terminus of Tel Aviv's famous beachfront promenade. Access is a short walk from several Tel Aviv bus routes and the Reading Power Station landmark. Water quality meets EU bathing water standards consistently. Surfing is common at the northern end, where exposure to open swell is greatest.

## 7. Hof HaPirtzah (Breach Beach), Acre North

Hof HaPirtzah lies just north of the ancient walled city of Acre, on a bay whose shallow crescent of water has been used since Phoenician times. The beach is gently shelving, unusually flat, and calmer than average owing to the protective arm of the Acre peninsula behind which it partly nestles. Water temperature lingers above 24°C from June through October. The combination of accessible warm water and the extraordinary backdrop of the Crusader-era Old City walls draws a mix of local Arab and Jewish families, as well as European visitors passing through as part of heritage tourism. Acre's Old City is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and worth pairing with any beach visit.

## 8. Kushi Beach, Haifa

Kushi Beach is a small urban beach in the central Haifa waterfront district, compact and busy during July and August but retaining a neighbourhood feel outside peak hours. It occupies a pocket of sand between marina infrastructure and a public promenade, with a fixed volleyball net and a seasonal kiosk. The water is clean, the bottom sandy, and the beach offers a direct view northward toward Acre and the Western Galilee hills — one of the most scenic coastal panoramas in Israel. Haifa's German Colony, a well-preserved 19th-century neighbourhood, is within walking distance, making Kushi a sensible base for an afternoon combining culture and bathing.

## 9. Hof Amnon, Kinneret

Hof Amnon departs from the Mediterranean pattern: it sits on the north-western shore of the Sea of Galilee (Lake Kinneret), Israel's main freshwater lake, 230 metres below sea level. The beach is part of a INPA nature reserve, set amid eucalyptus groves on a bay where the lake narrows. Freshwater bathing here is calm year-round; the lake's surface temperature reaches 28–30°C in summer. Birdlife in the adjacent wetlands is exceptional, with black-crowned night herons and kingfishers regularly visible. Day permits for the reserve are required but inexpensive. Entry is from Route 90 on the western Galilee shore road.

## 10. Hof Zevulun (Zevulun Beach), Haifa Bay North

Hof Zevulun anchors the north-eastern curve of Haifa Bay in the Zevulun Valley coastal plain. Named for the biblical tribe associated with seafaring, the beach is a broad, flat, wind-exposed strand popular with kitesurfers and windsurfers who exploit the bay's reliable westerly funnel effect. Summer conditions produce side-shore winds averaging 15–20 knots in the afternoons — excellent for board sports but requiring confident swimming ability for casual bathers. A small sailing club operates from the adjacent marina. The site is a 20-minute drive north of Haifa's city centre.

## Explore the Full Israeli Coast

The ten beaches above capture only a fraction of the variety along Israel's northern Mediterranean and lake coasts. Tucked coves beneath Carmel cliffs, unsupervised wild strands north of Nahariya, and small municipal beaches within walking distance of Crusader ruins all reward the patient explorer.
