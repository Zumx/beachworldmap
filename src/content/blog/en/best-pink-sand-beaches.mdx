Pink sand is a geological product of foraminifera — single-celled marine organisms with pink or red shells that live on the underside of coral reefs. When they die, their shells accumulate on the ocean floor, are broken down by wave action, and mix with white coral sand to produce the pink colouring. The colour ranges from a faint rose blush to a deep salmon pink depending on the concentration of foram shells and the whiteness of the surrounding sand. Most pink sand beaches are actually mildly pink rather than the vivid colour seen in photographs, which are almost always taken in the specific low-angle light of sunrise or sunset.

## 1. Pink Sands Beach, Harbour Island, Bahamas

Pink Sands Beach on Harbour Island (locally called Briland) is the most famous pink sand beach in the world. The beach runs for 5 kilometres along the Atlantic side of the island and genuinely has visible pink colouring visible in full daylight, not merely at golden hour. The Foraminifera that produce the colour are present in high enough concentrations to tint the sand clearly. Harbour Island is a short ferry from North Eleuthera and has a well-developed boutique resort economy. The beach is wide, the Atlantic surf is manageable, and the pink is real.

## 2. Spiaggia Rosa, Budelli, Sardinia, Italy

The Spiaggia Rosa on Budelli island in the La Maddalena Archipelago has been closed to landing since 1994 due to damage caused by visitors taking the pink sand as souvenirs. The beach can be viewed from the sea on boat tours from La Maddalena or Palau. The pink colouring comes from fragments of red coral, shells, and pink sandstone mixed with white quartz. The closure has allowed the beach to recover its colour to some extent. It remains the most photographed pink sand beach in Italy and one of the most photographed in Europe. Spiaggia Rosa is in the database.

## 3. Elafonisi, Crete, Greece

Elafonisi on Crete's southwest coast has a subtle pink hue to its sand in the eastern sections of the tidal island, produced by the same crushed Foraminifera and shell mechanism as other pink beaches. The pink is milder than Bermuda or Harbour Island, but the combination of the shallow turquoise water, the tidal island geography, and the pinkish sand at certain times of day makes it one of the most photographed Greek beaches. It is in the database and accessible from Chania in about an hour.

## 4. Tangsi Beach (Pink Beach), Lombok, Indonesia

Tangsi, known locally as Pink Beach, is on the southeast coast of Lombok. The pink colouring is derived from red coral fragments mixed into white sand. The beach has become more widely known as Lombok's tourist infrastructure has developed, and boat trips from Kuta Lombok or from Komodo include it on combined island-hopping itineraries. The beach faces a coral reef with good snorkelling offshore. The pink is visible and genuine, though more muted than the Bahamas reference.

## 5. Komodo Pink Beach, Komodo Island, Indonesia

Pink Beach on Komodo Island in East Nusa Tenggara is one of seven pink sand beaches in the world cited by National Geographic. The island is in Komodo National Park, accessible by boat from Labuan Bajo on Flores. The park visit typically includes a Komodo dragon encounter at the ranger station and a stop at Pink Beach for snorkelling. The coral reef offshore is healthy and the marine life exceptional. The beach's pink colour is genuine and visible in full daylight.

## 6. Horseshoe Bay, Bermuda

Bermuda's beaches have a characteristic pink colouring that extends beyond a single famous beach: it is a feature of the island's shoreline generally. The foraminifera responsible — Homotrema rubrum — is embedded in the island's geology and produces a consistent pink tint across most of Bermuda's sand beaches. Horseshoe Bay Beach in Southampton is the most visited and photographed, a horseshoe cove with Atlantic-clear water and pink sand. The beach has full facilities, lifeguards in season, and good food options.

## 7. Beaches of the Great Barrier Reef Cays, Australia

Some of the small coral cays in the outer Great Barrier Reef have pinkish sand from coral and Foraminifera. Lady Musgrave Island, accessible by catamaran from Bundaberg, has sand with a pink tint on sections of its beach. The cay is a national park with no permanent accommodation. Snorkelling directly from the beach onto an intact coral lagoon is the primary activity. The pink sand is subtle but the overall setting — a ring of sand in the middle of a turquoise coral lagoon — is among the most striking in Australia.

## 8. Crane Beach, Barbados

Crane Beach on the southeast coast of Barbados has a rose-tinted quality to its sand produced by coral sand mixing with the pale red volcanic rock fragments common in the area. The beach faces the Atlantic with a consistent surf break, backed by cliffs with the historic Crane Hotel at the top — one of the Caribbean's oldest resort hotels. The colour is subtle compared to the Bahamas but the overall setting — cliffs, turquoise water, the surf energy — makes it one of the best beaches in the Caribbean regardless of sand colour.

## Photography notes

Pink sand is most visible and most photogenic in three conditions: early morning or late afternoon low-angle light, wet sand immediately at the waterline, and when photographed against blue water or a contrasting backdrop. In mid-afternoon bright sunshine, many pink sand beaches look closer to pale beige to the naked eye. Managing expectations is important: the beaches are real and beautiful, but the vivid rose colour of many travel photographs requires specific light.

## What to expect on arrival

First-time visitors to pink sand beaches frequently express that the colour is less intense than photographs suggested. This is accurate for most pink sand beaches outside of Harbour Island, Bermuda, and Horseshoe Bay. The photographs are generally genuine — the colour is real — but capturing it requires the right conditions. The practical advice: visit early morning or late afternoon when low-angle light intensifies the colour. Look at the wet sand at the waterline where the foraminifera shells are visible when wet. Step away from the main crowd area to a drier part of the beach and crouch down to examine the individual grains: the pink shell fragments are visible at close range even when the overall effect appears merely pale at distance.

At Harbour Island, expectations are usually exceeded rather than disappointed. The beach is substantially pinker than almost any other pink sand beach at any time of day. At Elafonisi in Crete, the pink is more conditional — visible in good light on the eastern section of the beach, less so across the whole width and in overcast conditions. At Komodo Pink Beach, the colour is consistent and visible in full daylight, a genuine deep salmon pink rather than a photographic enhancement.

## Practical access summary

Harbour Island (Bahamas): fly to North Eleuthera, water taxi to Briland. Horseshoe Bay (Bermuda): direct flights from major US and UK cities, taxi or moped from Hamilton. Elafonisi (Crete): road access from Chania (1 hour). Komodo Pink Beach (Indonesia): boat from Labuan Bajo, typically included in Komodo Park day tours. Budelli/Spiaggia Rosa (Sardinia): boat tour from La Maddalena or Palau — no landing permitted. Lady Musgrave Island (Australia): catamaran day trip from Bundaberg.
